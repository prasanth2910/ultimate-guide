Description

Sanchi Stupa is a Buddhist complex, famous for its Great Stupa, located in Sanchi town, Madhya Pradesh, India. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the oldest stone structures in India, dating back to the 3rd century BCE. The stupa is a hemispherical structure containing relics of the Buddha and is surrounded by a railing with four gateways adorned with intricate carvings depicting scenes from the life of the Buddha.
